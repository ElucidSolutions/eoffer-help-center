Introduction

Welcome to the eOffer/eMod User Guide! This Guide will introduce you to the eOffer/eMod system, a web-based application designed to make the Contracting process efficient and secure. The system allows for seamless transmission of data from the Vendor/Contractor community to the Federal Acquisition (FAS) Contracting Offices. This Guide is intended for Vendors, but can be useful to Contracting Officers (COs) and Contracting Specialists (CSs) as well.

This Guide was written to give an accessible understanding of the different functions of eOffer/eMod to Vendors who use the system to submit Contract Offers and Contract Modifications. The Guide aims to give a comprehensive and easy to understand breakdown of the functions of eOffer/eMod, with helpful hints and important reminders along the way.

System Purpose and Overview

Under the Schedules Program, Vendors can establish long-term Contracts with GSA that allow them to do business with customers throughout the federal government. In order for Vendors to become a "GSA Schedule Contractor," they must first submit an Offer in response to an applicable Multiple Award Schedule Solicitation. These Solicitations are accessible at the Federal Business Opportunities website, (FBO.gov), or, as of 2011, via a link on the eOffer/eMod home page.

Launched in mid-2004, the eMod/eOffer system is a web-based interface allowing Vendors to electronically prepare and submit Contract Offers and Contract Modifications. At a high level, the system allows for an interactive, secure electronic environment that simplifies the Contracting process from Proposal preparation and submission through Contract Award and Closeout.

The eOffer module is available for more than 30 schedules, for example, Furniture (Schedule 71), Office Products and Supplies (Schedule 75), Financial and Business Solutions (Schedule 520), and Professional Engineering Services (Schedule 871).

The eMod module enables Contractors to prepare and submit Electronic Contract Modifications for all GSA MAS Contracts. Multiple eMods may be submitted within a single transaction, allowing for an expeditious process.

The eOffer/eMod system is online at http://eoffer.gsa.gov. Vendors can eSign any Contract Offers or Modifications, but must do so using their Digital Certificates.

The following five characteristics form the core benefits for a Vendor using the eOffer/eMod system:

  • eSimple – User friendly screens lead through the submittal process.

  • eSecure– Digital Certificates ensure the integrity of proprietary data.

  • eSave – Electronic process reduces delivery costs and delays.

  • eShare – Web-based technology facilitates collaboration on Proposal preparation.

  • eSign – Electronic signatures allow Offers and Mods to be signed quickly and easily.

System Requirements

In order to ensure the integrity and security of the eOffer/eMod system, four key requirements must be met before a Vendor can put the system to use. eOffer/eMod uses the latest digital authentication technology to ensure the integrity of data transmission, and to support the security of electronic signatures applied to Offer or Contract Modifications.

In order to log into the system, the following requirements must be met:

  1. Digital Certificate: A Digital Certificate is an electronic credential stored on the web browser that verifies the user's identity. It is required before a Vendor is granted access to the eOffer/eMod system.

  2. Data Universal Numbering System (DUNS) Number: The DUNS number is a unique nine digit identification number assigned to a business entity by Dun and Bradstreet. A valid DUNS number is required to use the eOffer/eMod system.

  3. System for Award Management (SAM): To use the eOffer/eMod system, users must have a DUNS number that is registered in the SAM database.

  4. "Pathway to Success" Training: The final requirement for participation in the MAS Schedules Program is successful completion of the "Pathway to Success" educational seminar.

Digital Certificates Overview

In order to successfully sign into the functional areas of eOffer/eMod, a Digital Certificate must be present and valid on the user's computer. In basic terms, Digital Certificates are electronic credentials that:

  • Assert the identity of an individual.

  • Enable eOffer/eMod to verify the identity of the individual accessing the system and signing documents.

  • Encrypt or decrypt data to ensure that it is securely transmitted.

  • Store accurate information directly within a web browser or within a token.

  • Create digital signatures that are verifiable.

A Digital Signature:

Is Is Not

  • A piece of information based on both the document and the signer's private key.

  • A digitized handwritten signature.

  • Unique to both the document and the signer.

  • The typed name of an individual

  • Virtually impossible to forge

  • A secret code, password, or a PIN.

Key Concept: Each individual accessing the eOffer/eMod system must have a Digital Certificate installed on his or her computer. Digital Certificates are unique to each person and cannot be used by another individual or a group.

System Validation of the Digital Certificate

Digital Certificates are required by Contractor staff in order to access the eOffer/eMod system. Upon logging into the system, Digital Certificate information is validated against the Authorized Negotiators on a Contract.

To begin, click on the Sign In button under Contract Offers (eOffers) or Contract Modification (eMod). When a user accesses either of the modules, there can be one of several outcomes:

  • The user's digital certificate is validated against the list of Authorized Negotiators on a Contract and the user is granted access.

  • Either the user has never been listed as an Authorized Negotiator because the original Offer was submitted by Paper and thus the user was never added to the electronic system, or the old Negotiator has left the Company at which point they are trying to get a new Negotiator listed in the table.

  • The user was in the Authorized Negotiator list and has either changed his or her name, or gotten a new Digital Certificate without listing his or her details as on the Authoized Negotiator list. Or the person who initially submitted the Vendor's information entered it into the system differently than how it was listed on their Digital Certificate.

If the user's login attempt falls into either of the last two scenarios, the system will attempt to validate by using certain combinations of user details. If a potential match is found in the Contract's list of Authorized Negotiators, the user will be able to select his or her information from a list provided, and then submit that information to the existing Authorized Negotiators/Contract Administrators for approval. If the existing Authorized Negotiators/Contract Administrators do not take an action with seven days to approve the new addition, the request is then sent to the CO/CS for approval. The Vendor's details are then transferred to the Authorized Negotiators/Contract Administrators, or to the CO/CS, for approval.

Preparing Your Electronic Contract Offer (eOffer) Overview

The web-based application eOffer allows Companies to electronically prepare and submit a Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) Contract Proposal to GSA's Federal Acquisition Service. It creates an interactive, secure electronic environment that simplifies the Contracting process from submission of Proposals all the way through Contract Award.

In the past, a Vendor's submission of a Contract Offer relied heavily on paper-based processes. Vendors would print a bunch of forms, compile their Offers, and mail them in to GSA. Email helped expedite the process somewhat, but GSA still lacked a centralized system capable of handing the entirety of the Offer process.

eOffer, introduced in May 2004, has become the preferred method by which Contract Offers are submitted to GSA. This system is available for use on most of GSA's Multiple Award Schedules (MAS). With a friendly user interface and advanced functionality, eOffer has become an important tool for GSA as well as the Vendor community.

Disaster Recovery Purchasing Program

Under the Disaster Recovery Purchasing Program, state and local government entities may purchase a variety of Products and Services from Contracts Awarded under GSA Federal Supply Schedules to facilitate recovery from a major disaster, terrorism, or nuclear, biological, chemical, or radiological attack.

State and local government entities may use GSA Schedule Contracts to purchase Products and Services in advance of a major disaster declared by the president, as well as in the aftermath of an emergency event. FAQ

How do I obtain a digital certificate?

  • A digital certificate can be obtained at http://eoffer.gsa.gov. Under the Before You Begin section, click on Digital Certificates. Once you have determined whether you want a certification from IdentTrust or Operational Research Consultants, click the corresponding link, read all information and click Get Certificate. Always remember your password, as GSA and the Digital Certificate Provider do not have access to it.

What is the Data Universal Number System Number?

  • A Data Universal Number System, or DUNS, number is a unique nine-digit identification number assigned to a business entity by Dun & Bradstreet. It is required to use the eOffer/eMod system and also needed when registering with the SAM database. The DUNS number assignment is free for all businesses required to register with the U.S. Federal government for contracts or grants.

How do I obtain a DUNS number?

  • Go to http://fedgov.dnb.com/webform. The Dun and Bradstreet homepage will be displayed. Click the option to request the DUNS number via the Web. From there you can either follow the instructions or select the option to request the number by phone.

How do I register with the System for Award Management?

  • You must create a User Account. Go to the SAM homepage: https://www.sam.gov/portal/public/SAM/. Click Create User Account. Choose either an Individual or System account. Under Individual Account Details, choose Create an Account. Simply follow all directions and fill out the Personal Information and Account information form fields. You will be asked to confirm and submit all information upon completion.

How do I attend a Pathway to Success Training presentation?

  • Vendors may attend either a live presentation or complete the Web-based presentation posted on the Vendor Support Center at http://vsc.gsa.gov under the Vendor Training tab.
